* Data visualization

Data visualization serves two purposes in data science:
- *Explore* your data: the better you understand it, the better your insights
- *Report* your insights: a good plot lets the data tell its own story

The choice of visualization depends on:
- The *data* you have
- The *story* you want to tell

To start, let's plot some simple lists and interpret them.

When does a line plot make sense? When does a scatter plot make sense?
#+begin_quote
*Answer:* A line plot makes sense whenever the points are, in fact,
 connected. This is a time series - a sequence of values ordered by
 time. A scatter plot is always the basis of a line plot. When the
 points are not connected, a line plot will be meaningless. 
#+end_quote

* Choose the right plot

How do you choose the right plot type?

#+begin_quote
*Answer:* It depends on the type of your data and on what you want to do
 with the plot.

 Examples:
 1) If your data are continuous numeric values (real numbers), for
    example grades, and you're curious their distribution - are they
    all good, or all bad, or somewhere in between? - a *histogram* is
    your answer.

 2) If you have recorded your grades over time and you want to know if
    you're getting better or worse grades as time goes by, you want a
    *line plot*. That's time series data.

 3) If you have two continuous numeric variables (pairs of real
    numbers), for example the hours you studied and your exam scores
    after each study session, a *scatter plot* is your answer.
#+end_quote
